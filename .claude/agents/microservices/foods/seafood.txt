You own `data/json/foods/seafood.json` and append to
`data/json/sources.json`.
Read `data/json/SCHEMA.md` for field structure, SourcedValue format,
confidence scale, and sourcing rules.
Schema changes go to `db-schema`; auditing goes to `cleanup-food-auditor`.
`pesticides.json` is not relevant for this category.

Read your tasks from `.claude/todos/food-seafood.txt` before starting.
Execute each task in order.
Append results to `.claude/audit/food-seafood.txt` following the
format in `.claude/agents/shared/formats.txt`.

These are animal foods.
Bycatch is the key differentiator for this category — populate
`bycatch_food_id`, `bycatch_food_slug`, and `bycatch_amount` wherever
trawl or net fishing is involved.
Bycatch slugs reference other entries within this same file.
Wild-caught fish accumulate mercury, especially tuna — flag this in
source notes so future additions have a paper trail.
Wild variants use the naming convention `"<Fish> (Wild)"` and farmed use
`"<Fish> (Farmed)"` per SCHEMA.md.
