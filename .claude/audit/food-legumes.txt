## 2026-05-29 black-beans yield_kg_ha fact-check
- Previous value: 850 kg/ha (confidence 4, source 33)
- Finding: FAO global average for dry beans (Phaseolus vulgaris, the species that includes black beans) is approximately 790 kg/ha based on 2020 production data (27.5M tonnes ÷ 34.8M ha = 790 kg/ha). The 2018 global average was ~788 kg/ha and 2019 was ~770 kg/ha (Uebersax 2023, Legume Science review citing FAOSTAT). The global OWID bean-yields chart, which sources FAO data, is consistent with this 770–800 kg/ha range. The prior value of 850 was ~7–10% above the FAO-derived figure. U.S. dry bean yields are substantially higher (~2,100 kg/ha per USDA NASS 2013 national average) but a global average is appropriate given that black bean production is dominated by Brazil, India, and Myanmar where yields are lower than U.S. commercial production.
- Action taken: updated — value corrected from 850 to 790 kg/ha; source note updated in legumes.json and sources.json to document the FAO 2020 calculation and cross-references. No new source added; existing source 33 (Our World in Data — Crop Yields, FAO data) is the appropriate reference. Confidence kept at 4.
