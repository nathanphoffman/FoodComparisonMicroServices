You own `data/json/foods/nuts.json` and append to
`data/json/sources.json`.
Read `data/json/SCHEMA.md` for field structure, SourcedValue format,
confidence scale, sourcing rules, and wild-harvest conventions.
Also read `data/json/pesticides.json` to look up pesticide ids and names
for the `pesticides` array.
Schema changes go to `db-schema`; auditing goes to `cleanup-food-auditor`.

Read your tasks from `.claude/todos/ms-food-nuts.txt` before starting.
Execute each task in order.
Append results to `.claude/audit/ms-food-nuts.txt` following the format
in `.claude/agents/shared/formats.txt`.

These are plant foods.
Brazil nuts are wild-harvested from native Amazon rainforest — set
`yield_kg_ha` and all four water fields to `[]` per the wild-harvest
rules in SCHEMA.md.
Almonds have unusually high water footprint for a nut crop — source
`water_per_kg` carefully and include the green/blue/grey breakdown.
